Giuseppe Tornatore

Giuseppe Tornatore was born May 27th, 1956 in Sicily.
He put on his first productions at the age of 16, adaptions of the works of Luigi Pirandello and Eduardo De Filippo. He started his carrer as a freelance photographer before moving into cinema. His first work was a collaboration on "The Ethnic Minorities of Sicily". In 1985 he won a Silver Ribbon for best new director for his first full-length film "Il Camorrista". 1988 saw his greatest achievement with the release of "Nouvo Cinema Paridiso". The story of a successful director returning to a village in Sicily for the funeral of his mentor. The film won world-wide acclaim and recieved the Acadamy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.
Every film he has released since has obviously been compared to Nouvo Cinema Paridiso and usually un favourably so. In 2007 he did win the Silver George for Best Director at the 29th Moscow International Film Awards for the film "The Unknown Woman".
